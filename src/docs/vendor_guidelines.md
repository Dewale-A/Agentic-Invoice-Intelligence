# Vendor Management Guidelines

## 1. Vendor Onboarding Requirements

Before a vendor can receive payment, the following must be completed:

- Completed vendor registration form with legal entity name and tax ID.
- Certificate of insurance (where applicable).
- Signed master services agreement or contract on file.
- Banking details submitted through the secure vendor portal.
- Approval by Procurement and Finance.

Invoices from vendors not in the approved registry are automatically held.

## 2. Approved Vendor Categories

The following vendor categories are recognized:

- **Technology**: Software, SaaS, cloud infrastructure, hosting.
- **Professional Services**: Consulting, advisory, project management.
- **Infrastructure**: Networking, hardware, data center.
- **Security**: Penetration testing, security audits, monitoring.
- **Staffing**: Contractors, temp staff, recruitment.
- **Facilities**: Cleaning, maintenance, office management.
- **Analytics**: BI tools, data services, reporting.
- **Legal**: Legal counsel, compliance advisory.
- **Marketing**: Digital marketing, creative, PR.
- **Audit**: Internal and external audit services.
- **Creative**: Design, branding, content.
- **Compliance**: Regulatory compliance, risk management.
- **HR**: Payroll, HR systems, benefits administration.
- **Logistics**: Courier, shipping, fleet management.
- **Office Supplies**: Stationery, printing, consumables.
- **Payroll**: Payroll processing services.
- **Training**: Staff training, e-learning, certification.

## 3. Contracted Rates

All vendors with negotiated contracted rates are recorded in the vendor registry. Invoices that exceed contracted rates by more than 5% require:

- Written explanation from the vendor.
- Sign-off from the sponsoring business unit.
- Updated contract amendment if the rate change is permanent.

## 4. Vendor Performance and Compliance

Vendors are reviewed annually on the following criteria:

- Invoice accuracy rate (target: >95% invoices pass first-time validation).
- Average invoice quality score (based on OCR confidence and field completeness).
- Compliance with payment terms (correct PO references, valid invoice numbers).

Vendors with invoice accuracy below 80% over a 6-month period are flagged for remediation coaching.

## 5. Vendor Suspension Policy

A vendor account is suspended if:

- Fraudulent invoice activity is detected (duplicate submissions, inflated amounts).
- The vendor fails to respond to duplicate invoice investigations within 10 business days.
- The vendor's insurance or certifications expire without renewal.

Suspended vendors cannot receive payments until the suspension is lifted by Procurement.

## 7. Invoice Submission Requirements for Vendors

Vendors must submit invoices that include:

1. Legal vendor name matching the registered name exactly.
2. Unique invoice number (not reused within 12 months).
3. Reference to the relevant purchase order number.
4. Line-item breakdown with description, quantity, unit price, and subtotal.
5. Applicable taxes shown separately.
6. Bank details or payment instructions (if first invoice or banking change).

Electronic invoices in PDF format are strongly preferred. Image-only scans cause processing delays.
